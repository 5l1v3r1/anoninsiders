Anon Insiders Tales : Operation Justice for Rehtaeh Parsons

April 2013 -Special Edition

This is the sad story of another girl who saw her life destroyed by rapists in the small community of Cole Harbour, Nova Scotia. Literally destroyed when she took her own life. Turns out Anonymous does not take kindly to rape and cyberbullying and the collective grabbed its Sherlock Holmes cape. The game was afoot very briefly. They were allegedly able to identify the rapists in 24 hours and told the police they could help.

But the police didn’t like it at all…
Police : “If Anonymous want to work with us then they better drop their masks.”
Anons : “If the police want to work with us then they better put on a mask.”

The #lulz!

They don’t like it? So what! Let’s push it further and organize a protest in front of their HQ! YAR!

So the story continues in Halifax, during the demo caught by livestreamers. Protesters chanted “Cops, do your job.” while Tim Horton was giving free coffee to the protesters. Even Rehtaeh’s mother was there. The public was afraid that we screw up but the operation team did a great job and put up the most amazing PR I ever saw. Big GuyFawkes hug to all anons on the team and supporters.

In the end we got our private indy investigation approved, the case reopened, Rehtaeh’s parents meet the Prime Minister and  Rehtaeh’s father thanked us. Even the Halifax school board is to be reviewed. HAHA! Then we were called many things: vigilantes or heroes? Whichever suits you best folks! Both are fine with us.

The police may still busy itself recovering 2,000$ of stolen dolls, cologne and after-shave, but at least the Nova Scotia Minister of Justice Ross Landry, after some coaxing from your Anons truly,  seemed to have a sudden change of heart. Anyone feel like PR consulting?
