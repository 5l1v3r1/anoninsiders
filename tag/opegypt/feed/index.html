To The People Of The World,

Greetings From Anonymous.

On the 25th Of January the citizens of Egypt once again took to the streets in record numbers to commemorate the 2nd anniversary of the Egyptian uprising that resulted in the overthrow of the Mubarak dictatorship and to voice their anger and discontent with the current regime being led by the Muslim Brotherhood and Mohamed Morsi.

Morsi’s regime had completely failed in their promise to deliver democracy to the people of Egypt and the same corruption, brutality, lack of democratic freedoms and widespread human rights abuses that existed during the Mubarak era still continues under Muslim Brotherhood rule.

In true dictator style, Morsi unleashed his brutal thugs from the security forces as well as armed plainclothed Muslim Brotherhood members and rifle-toting snipers on the huge numbers of unarmed peaceful protesters who had gathered in Tahrir Square in Cairo, outside the presidential palace in Cairo and in many other locations across Egypt.

Anonymous watched in absolute horror as our Egyptian brothers and sisters were literally being shot, stabbed, beaten, tear-gassed, dragged away to detention centers and even sexually assaulted by Morsi’s thugs – many protesters were killed, some who were killed were not even taking part in any protests. The killing and the brutality that took place was no different than what took place under the Mubarak regime, if anything it was a continuation, so much for Morsi and his promises of democracy for all Egyptians!

Anonymous could not sit back and idly watch as our brothers and sisters in Egypt were facing such shocking, savage & inhumane treatment for the so-called ‘crime’ of gathering in the streets to peacefully demand basic democratic rights. We decided to act in solidarity with the people of Egypt by banding together many dedicated Anons from all corners of the globe and and co-ordinating some electronic civil disobedience!

For the period covering the 30th of January 2013 to the 2nd of February, Anonymous : #OpEgypt claim full responsibility for the electronic civil disobedience actions via Distributed Denial Of Service (DDOS) attacks on the following Egyptian government & Muslim Brotherhood related websites…

  • English-language website for the Egyptian Ministry of the Interior (at the time of writing of this press release (Feb 03 2013), this site is still not available worldwide)
  • A Muslim Brotherhood propaganda website, which was forced offline for over half an hour.
  • Egyptian Ministry of Information website, which was forced offline for over half an hour.
  • Egyptian Ministry Of Communications & Technology website, which was also forced offline for over half an hour.
  • Egypt’s Cabinet website.

For screengrabs that document these actions please check the Facebook event page and in particular the following photo album that can be found on the event page where you will also find many graphic presentations relevant to the operation

As this press release is being written, the protests in Egypt are continuing, as is the violent & barbaric crackdown by Morsi’s security forces. The regime is now attempting to disrupt unity among the protesters by demonizing certain groups of protesters by labelling them as ‘terrorist groups’, particularly groups such as the Black Bloc who were brave enough to physically protect citizens under attack from the security forces and to physically resist the vicious attacks from Morsi’s thugs.

Shocking video footage has also emerged from the past few days showing the horrendous violence that protestors are now facing at the hands of the security forces, this is just one of many but please be warned, this footage is quite confronting…

This phase of #OpEgypt is fully operational at this stage until the 28th of February, but if the situation in Egypt continues at it’s present rate, then you can definitely EXPECT US to continue!!

To keep up to date with events in Egypt please be sure to follow the #OpEgypt twitter account : @OperationEgypt
You can also find #OpEgypt on Facebook.
