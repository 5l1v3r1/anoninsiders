What the f*** is going on

On December 1, 2013, Yanukovych and his MVS/Berkut thugs unleashed a brutal crackdown on journalists and media that are not sympathetic to his interests and ambitions. According to an incredibly detailed report by Telekritica, over 50 journalists were targetted and injured by the Berkut (Ukrainian version) during the protests. Many of the reporters were beaten by the Berkut, despite displaying proper press credentials and informing the Berkut that they were members of the press. Additionally, the office of the opposition party, Batkivshchyna, was raided by an MVS unit armed with machine guns on December 9. The excuse used by the MVS was that they were provided tips regarding stolen computer equipment in the office. During the raid, the MVS destroyed or seized multiple servers. On the same day, the MVS also destroyed or seized the servers of numerous press entities, including the newspaper Vechirni Visti, the online TV station INTV and the news website Cenzor.net. During these raids, the MVS wore masks and attempted to operate under the shield of anonymity, all in an effort to send a threat to both the media and the public.

In the face of a deliberate and inexcusable effort by Yanukovych and the MVS to punish both the people and the media for merely reporting what is happening in Ukraine, we will rise to the occassion to offer everyone, the people and the media, the ability to make sure their footage is spread as quickly and as efficiently as possible as well as being preserved. Unlike the servers located in Ukraine, our servers and network are beyond the reach and intimidation of Yanukovych and anonymous MVS hooligans. Since the MVS thinks they can act under a cloak of anonymity to intimidate the people and silence the media, we now offer you the power of Anonymous to communicate with the world and create the media. We are here to help everyone show that Yanukovych's efforts in bullying the people into silence have been, and will continue to be, in vain. Join us!

A note about media

If you will be supplying us with media that you've taken with your cellular phone, consider the following steps:

For Android based device, use Orbot from The Guardian Project for your network communications. This is the official port of Tor for Android devices. The Guardian Project has provided an interactive guide to walk you through installing Orbot on your cellular phone Unfortunately, there is no official Tor port for the iPhone at the moment.

Disable geotagging on your phone! If you don't, GPS coordinates of the picture will be stored in your images. This can be used to locate and/or identify you. Read how to disable geotagging.

We will remove data from your pictures unless you ask us not to. However, it's safest to remove it yourself. Tools you can use https://mat.boum.org/ (works on numerous types of files) and http://atomicparsley.sourceforge.net/ (works on MP4 files).
